EARTHQUAKE

“Duck, Cover, Hold” — pero ano pagkatapos?

Practical checklist para sa bahay at opisina

Ang lindol madalas biglaan. Kaya mas okay na may simple plan kaysa complicated na hindi nasusunod. Kung tapos na ang pagyanig:

After-shake mini checklist
  • Check injuries. First aid agad kung kailangan.
  • Look for hazards: gas leak, sparks, falling debris.
  • If building is unsafe, evacuate calmly via stairs.
  • Text > call. Keep lines open for emergencies.
“Mas mahalaga ang practice kaysa perfect plan.”

Tip sa office setup: magtalaga ng meeting point sa labas at kung sino ang magche-check ng attendance. Kahit simple lang, malaking tulong kapag may panic.

TYPHOON

Bagyo: Handa bago pa lumakas ang hanging
Go-bag + bahay checklist

Sa bagyo, maraming oras para maghanda. Sulitin yung time bago lumakas ang ulan. Prioritize: kuryente, tubig, pagkain, communication.

If baha-prone ang area: iangat ang appliances, maghanda ng waterproof pouch para sa documents, at i-charge ang powerbank.

Before
  • Check roof & drainage
  • Charge devices
  • Stock water/snacks
During
  • Stay indoors
  • Monitor advisories
  • Avoid flood waters
After
  • Inspect wires
  • Boil water if unsure
  • Document damage
FIRE

Sunog: exits, smoke, at “small fire” basics

Safety reminders na madaling tandaan

Kung may usok, stay low at lumabas agad. Huwag magtago. Kung maliit pa lang ang apoy at may training, doon lang gumamit ng extinguisher.

Do
  • Know your exits
  • Keep door keys accessible
  • Practice a quick evacuation route
Don’t
  • Use elevators
  • Open hot doors
  • Re-enter for items

Fire Prevention

  • Turn off stoves when not used
  • Replace damaged cords
  • Unplug appliances when not used
  • Never leave open flames unattended
  • Install smoke alarms
  • Make sure to have a fire extinguisher nearby
FLOOD

Baha: iwas-disgrasya sa tubig na akala mo “mababaw lang”

Safety checklist para sa kalsada at bahay

Kahit mababaw ang baha, puwedeng may open manhole, matutulis na debris, at kuryente mula sa exposed wires. Kung may option na umiwas, umiwas.

Quick Flood Rules
  • Don’t wade kung hindi kailangan. Prioritize safety over shortcut.
  • Kung nasa bahay: ilipat sa mataas ang appliances at documents.
  • Patayin ang main switch kung may tubig na papasok at ligtas gawin.
  • Iwasan ang mabilis na agos — kahit mababaw, puwedeng itumba.
If you must pass through
  • Use flashlight at tingnan ang daan.
  • Wear rubber slippers/boots.
  • Hold on to a stable support.
After the flood
  • Disinfect surfaces; wash hands often.
  • Boil water if supply is uncertain.
  • Watch for fever/skin wounds; seek care if needed.
ESSENTIALS

Go-bag: what to pack

  • Water (3 days)
  • Ready-to-eat snacks
  • Flashlight + batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Powerbank + cable
  • IDs (photocopy)
  • Whistle, face mask
Tip: rotate supplies every 3–6 months.

FIRST AID

Basics You Can Do Right Away

Simple steps. Kapag malala, tumawag agad sa 911 at pumunta sa ospital.

🩹

Bleeding

  1. Wash hands / sanitize.
  2. Apply direct pressure (clean cloth/gauze).
  3. Elevate if possible.
  4. Seek help if bleeding won’t stop.
🔥

Burns

  1. Cool running water 10–20 mins.
  2. Cover with clean non-stick dressing.
  3. Don’t pop blisters.
  4. Go to hospital if large/severe.
🦴

Sprain / Fracture

  1. Immobilize area (do not “set” bone).
  2. Cold pack 10–15 mins.
  3. Elevate when possible.
  4. Medical evaluation ASAP.
😵

Fainting

  1. Lay down, raise legs slightly.
  2. Loosen clothing, get fresh air.
  3. Check breathing. Call emergency if needed.
  4. Let them sit slowly, hydrate.

Note: CPR requires training. If someone collapses and isn’t breathing normally, call 911 and follow dispatcher instructions.
